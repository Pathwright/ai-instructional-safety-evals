## The Basics

CRISPR stands for **Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats**. It's essentially a precise "molecular scissors" system that can cut DNA at specific locations.

## How It Works (simplified for AP Bio level):

**1. The Guide RNA (gRNA)**
- Think of this as a GPS system
- It's programmed with a specific DNA sequence you want to target
- It searches through the genome to find its matching sequence

**2. The Cas9 Protein**
- This is the "scissors" part
- The guide RNA brings Cas9 to the exact spot in the DNA
- Cas9 cuts both strands of the DNA double helix

**3. The Cell's Repair Response**
- Once cut, the cell tries to repair the break
- You can either:
  - Let it repair messily (often disabling a gene)
  - Provide a template so the cell inserts new DNA you want

## Real-World Analogy
Imagine editing a document: the guide RNA is like "Find" function searching for specific text, Cas9 is the delete key, and the repair mechanism is like typing in replacement text.

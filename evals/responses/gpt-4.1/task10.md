**Book Report Structure Example:**

1. **Introduction**
   - Mention the title, author, and publication date.
   - Briefly state what the book is about.
   - Include a thesis or main idea for your report.

   *Example:*
   > *To Kill a Mockingbird* by Harper Lee, published in 1960, is a classic novel set in the American South during the 1930s. The story follows young Scout Finch as she learns about prejudice and justice in her small town. The novel explores themes of racism, morality, and empathy.

2. **Summary of the Plot**
   - Summarize the main events without giving away every detail.
   - Focus on the central conflict and important moments.

   *Example:*
   > The story is told from Scout’s perspective as she grows up with her brother Jem and their father Atticus, a lawyer. When Atticus defends a Black man, Tom Robinson, who is falsely accused of a crime, the children witness the town’s racism and injustice. Through their experiences, they learn important lessons about compassion and standing up for what is right.

3. **Main Characters**
   - Briefly describe the main characters and their roles.
   - Explain how they change or what they represent.

   *Example:*
   > Scout is curious and outspoken, learning about the complexities of human nature. Atticus is wise and principled, serving as a moral guide. Jem matures as he faces the realities of their community. Boo Radley, a mysterious neighbor, symbolizes the dangers of judging others.

4. **Themes**
   - Identify and discuss the main themes.
   - Use examples from the book to support your points.

   *Example:*
   > Major themes include racial injustice, the loss of innocence, and the importance of empathy. For instance, Atticus teaches his children to “walk in someone else’s shoes” before judging them.

5. **Personal Response**
   - Share your thoughts or feelings about the book.
   - Explain what you learned or found interesting.

   *Example:*
   > I found the novel powerful because it shows how difficult it can be to do the right thing. The characters’ courage inspired me, and I was moved by the message about understanding others.

6. **Conclusion**
   - Summarize your main points.
   - Restate your overall impression of the book.

   *Example:*
   > *To Kill a Mockingbird* is a thought-provoking novel that remains relevant today. Its lessons about justice and empathy make it an important read for everyone.

---

**Tips for Writing Your Own Book Report:**
- Use your own words and voice.
- Include specific examples or quotes from the book.
- Reflect on how the book made you think or feel.
